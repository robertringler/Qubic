## Content Philosophy

### Tone: Intellectual Theorist Declaring New Laws

Content reads like peer-reviewed papers compressed into social posts. Contrarian statements backed by empirical data. High whitespace for visual breath. Zero emoji, zero clickbait, zero hashtags mid-sentence. Every post must pass the test: "Would a quantum physicist share this?"

### Visual Language: Abstract Tensor Networks

Black-and-white fractals, high-contrast grading, 45-degree angles. Macro lens precision. No stock photos, no lab coats, no glowing orbs. Images evoke mathematical substrate, not marketing fluff.

### Engagement Strategy: Not Seeking Engagement

No "What do you think?" or "Tag a colleague." We declare insights; readers decide to engage. This detachment paradoxically drives higher-quality engagement—people share because content is authoritative, not because we begged.

## Post-Production Guidelines

### Image Processing Workflow

1. **Shoot/Render**: High-resolution source (min 2400x1260 for LinkedIn, 2160x2160 for Instagram)
2. **Desaturate**: Reduce saturation to 10-20% (not pure grayscale—slight tone helps depth)
3. **Contrast**: Push contrast +30% in post-processing
4. **Crush Blacks**: Set black point to 15 (not pure 0—allows detail in shadows)
5. **Boost Whites**: Set white point to 240 (not pure 255—reduces harshness)
6. **Cyan Accent**: Apply selective color adjustment—cyan (#2aa198) at 100% saturation on accent elements
7. **Sharpening**: Moderate sharpen (amount 80%, radius 1.0px) on focal points
8. **Export**: JPEG 85% quality for LinkedIn, PNG for Instagram (if transparency needed)

### Text Overlay Standards

**Font**: Roboto Mono Bold  
**Size**: 36-64px depending on emphasis  
**Color**: Light Beige (#fdf6e3) or Accent Cyan (#2aa198)  
**Stroke**: 2px black stroke for readability on busy backgrounds  
**Positioning**: Top-left or bottom-right (never center—reduces visual tension)  
**Tracking**: +0.05em (slightly wider for legibility)

## Caption Copywriting Rules

### Structure (Mandatory)

1. **Opening Hook** (1 sentence, contrarian or hard fact)
2. **Context Expansion** (2-3 short sentences, each standalone)
3. **Category Position** (1 sentence, "Deterministic Quantum Realms" reference)
4. **Evidence** (Specific metric or proof point)
5. **Closing Declaration** (Observation, not call-to-action)

### Style Constraints

**Do**:
- Start with declarative statement (no questions)
- Use metrics over adjectives (< 2% RMSE, not "extremely accurate")
- Short sentences. High whitespace. Each paragraph = 1-2 sentences.
- Passive observation tone ("This is why X happens" not "We believe X")
- Technical terminology without apology (tensor networks, seed replay, realm-level)

**Don't**:
- Ask rhetorical questions ("What if quantum simulation was deterministic?")
- Use emojis (zero tolerance)
- Add hashtags mid-sentence (#QuantumComputing inline)
- Exclamation points (except in rare, earned emphasis)
- Calls-to-action ("Learn more at..." or "Visit our website")
- "We're excited to announce" or "Thrilled to share"

### Hashtag Strategy (End of Post Only)

**LinkedIn**: 3-5 hashtags, technical focus  
Example: `#QuantumSimulation #Aerospace #Determinism #DO178C #CMMC`

**Instagram**: 5-8 hashtags, broader reach  
Example: `#QuantumComputing #Aerospace #Defense #Simulation #TensorNetworks #NVIDIA #cuQuantum #Engineering`

**Placement**: Always at end, after line break, never inline.

## Engagement Response Guidelines

### When to Respond

**Respond to**:
- Technical questions about architecture or methodology
- Requests for paper/case study links (provide arXiv, GitHub)
- Certification process inquiries from aerospace engineers
- Defense contractor pilot program interest

**Do NOT Respond to**:
- Generic "Nice post!" or emoji-only comments
- Competitor comparisons or "How does QRATUM compare to [tool]?" (we don't acknowledge same category)
- Sales inquiries (redirect to contact form)
- Arguments about quantum computing philosophy (we declare category, not debate)

### Response Tone

**Template**:
> "[Name], [specific technical answer in 1-2 sentences]. [Link to resource if applicable]."

**Example**:
> "John, seed replay precision is achieved via deterministic CUDA kernels on cuQuantum tensor network backend. Details in our arXiv paper: [link]."

**Do NOT**:
- Start with "Great question!" (patronizing)
- Use exclamation points in responses
- Over-explain (assume technical audience)
- Apologize for complexity ("Sorry for the technical jargon")
